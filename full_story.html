The Summary of the Ramayana

The Ramayana, one of the greatest epics in Indian literature, tells the divine story of Lord Ram, an avatar of Vishnu, and his journey of heroism, devotion, and righteousness. Below is the summary of the seven Kandas and the lessons learnt from each.

Bala Kanda

Summary: The Bala Kanda narrates the origins of Lord Rama and his three brothers: Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. King Dasharatha of Ayodhya, who had three queens—Kausalya, Kaikeyi, and Sumitra—remained childless until performing a sacred fire ritual, the Putrakameshti Yagya. This led to the birth of the four princes, with Rama born to Kausalya, Bharata to Kaikeyi, and Lakshmana and Shatrughna to Sumitra. The book details their upbringing, education in scriptures, and training in warfare. Notable events include Rama and Lakshmana aiding Sage Vishwamitra by defeating demons that hindered his sacrifices. Rama, chosen by Vishwamitra, showed extraordinary valor at just 16 years old. The section also explores Sita’s divine origins—discovered in a field by King Janaka of Mithila—and her marriage to Rama, who proved his worth by lifting and stringing Lord Shiva’s bow. The Bala Kanda concludes with the weddings of Rama to Sita, Lakshmana to Urmila, Bharata to Mandavi, and Shatrughna to Shrutakirti.

  • Rama and his brothers are born through a divine ritual.
  • Rama and Lakshmana accompany Vishwamitra to vanquish demons.
  • Rama wins Sita's hand by lifting Shiva's bow.

Lesson: The Bala Kanda emphasizes humility, duty, and respect for elders.

Ayodhya Kanda

Summary: The Ayodhya Kanda chronicles the events leading to Lord Rama's exile to the forest for fourteen years. After twelve years of marriage, King Dasharatha decides to crown Rama as his successor, a choice celebrated by the citizens and the royal assembly. However, on the eve of Rama's coronation, Kaikeyi, influenced by her scheming maid Manthara, demands two boons previously promised by Dasharatha: Rama's exile for fourteen years and the throne for her son Bharata. Bound by his word, Dasharatha reluctantly agrees. Rama, embodying humility and obedience, accepts his fate with grace. Despite Rama’s initial refusal, Sita insists on accompanying him, displaying her unwavering love and devotion. Lakshmana also chooses to join them in exile. Bharata, upon learning of the deceit, refuses the throne and instead places Rama's sandals on it, ruling on Rama's behalf until his return.

  • Dasharatha plans Rama's coronation, but Kaikeyi intervenes.
  • Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana leave for the forest.
  • Bharata declines the throne and rules in Rama's name.

Lesson: Loyalty, selflessness, and adherence to dharma are central themes.

Aranya Kanda

Summary: The Aranya Kanda recounts pivotal events during the last year of Rama's exile. The narrative begins with Rama defeating Khara and his demon forces, retaliating against an attack triggered by Lakshmana injuring Surpanakha, Ravana’s sister. The most significant event is Sita's abduction by Ravana after she steps outside the protective Lakshmana Rekha to aid a disguised Ravana. Taken to Lanka, Sita resists Ravana’s advances and remains steadfast in her devotion to Rama. Meanwhile, Rama and Lakshmana, learning of her abduction from the dying vulture Jatayu, set out in pursuit. Along the way, they encounter Kabandha and Shabari, who guide them to Sugriva and Hanuman, marking the beginning of a powerful alliance.

  • Rama defeats Khara and his demon army.
  • Sita is abducted by Ravana after crossing the Lakshmana Rekha.
  • Rama and Lakshmana meet Hanuman and Sugriva in their quest to rescue Sita.

Lesson: Courage, resilience, and devotion shine through in the face of adversity.

Kishkindha Kanda

Summary: The Kishkindha Kanda focuses on Rama’s alliance with the vanara king Sugriva. Rama and Lakshmana meet Hanuman, Sugriva's loyal companion, who introduces them to Sugriva. Rama assists Sugriva by defeating his elder brother, Vali, enabling Sugriva to reclaim his throne. In return, Sugriva pledges to help Rama find Sita. Initially distracted from his promise, Sugriva is reminded by his wife Tara of his duty to Rama. Sugriva then dispatches search parties in all directions. The southern team, led by Hanuman and Angad, discovers from Sampati, a vulture, that Sita is imprisoned in Lanka.

  • Rama meets Hanuman and forms an alliance with Sugriva.
  • Sugriva regains his throne with Rama's help.
  • Hanuman learns of Sita’s location in Lanka.

Lesson: Friendship, loyalty, and perseverance are crucial for overcoming challenges.

Sundara Kanda

Summary: The Sundara Kanda is a vivid narration of Hanuman’s extraordinary journey to Lanka. Demonstrating his immense strength and devotion, Hanuman transforms into a colossal form to leap across the ocean. Facing numerous challenges from demons testing his power, Hanuman overcomes every obstacle to reach Lanka and locate Sita. His arrival causes chaos as he destroys trees, buildings, and Ravana’s soldiers. Intentionally captured to speak with Ravana, Hanuman appeals for Sita's release, but Ravana refuses and orders Hanuman’s tail to be set ablaze. Using this to his advantage, Hanuman leaps across rooftops, setting Ravana’s citadel on fire. Victorious, he returns to Kishkindha with news of Sita’s location.

  • Hanuman leaps across the ocean to Lanka.
  • He causes havoc in Ravana’s kingdom and meets Sita.
  • Hanuman returns with critical information for Rama.

Lesson: Devotion, courage, and resourcefulness lead to success.

Yuddha Kanda

Summary: The Yuddha Kanda details the climactic war between Rama and Ravana. The story begins with the construction of the Rama Setu bridge, built from floating stones inscribed with Rama’s name, allowing Rama’s army to cross to Lanka. Ravana’s brother Vibhishana allies with Rama. The epic also recounts Hanuman’s heroic act of carrying Mount Sumeru to save Lakshmana, injured by a weapon wielded by Indrajit, Ravana’s son. The war culminates in Rama’s victory as he kills Ravana and crowns Vibhishana as Lanka’s king. Sita undergoes the Agni Pareeksha to prove her purity, emerging unscathed. With the war over and the exile completed, Rama returns to Ayodhya amidst grand celebrations.

  • Rama’s army builds the Rama Setu to reach Lanka.
  • Rama defeats Ravana, ending the war.
  • Sita proves her purity through the Agni Pareeksha.

Lesson: Triumph of good over evil and adherence to dharma.

Uttara Kanda

Summary: The Uttara Kanda narrates the later years of Rama, Sita, and their family. Despite proving her purity, Sita is exiled to the forest due to public opinion. Under the care of Sage Valmiki, she gives birth to twins, Lava and Kusha, who are raised with noble teachings. Valmiki composes the Ramayana, which Lava and Kusha recite before Rama. Overcome with sorrow upon hearing Sita’s story, Rama witnesses Sita’s return to the earth, her birthplace. Rama later learns Lava and Kusha are his sons. After fulfilling his earthly mission, Rama ascends to his heavenly abode, marking the end of his mortal journey.

  • Sita is exiled and gives birth to Lava and Kusha.
  • Valmiki composes the Ramayana, sung by Lava and Kusha.
  • Sita returns to the earth, and Rama ascends to Vaikuntha.

Lesson: The complexity of dharma and the inevitability of destiny are central themes.
