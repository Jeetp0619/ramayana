The Summary of the Ramyana

The Ramyana, one of the greatest epics in Indian literature, tells the divine story of Lord Ram, an avatar of Vishnu, and his journey of heroism, devotion, and righteousness. Below is the summary of the seven Kandas and the lessons learnt from each.

Bala Kanda

Summary: The Bala Kanda narrates the origins of Lord Ram and his three brothers: Lakshman, Bharat, and Shatrughna. King Dasharath of Ayodhya, who had three queens—Kausalya, Kaikeyi, and Sumitra—remained childless until performing a sacred fire ritual, the Putrakameshti Yagya. This led to the birth of the four princes, with Ram born to Kausalya, Bharat to Kaikeyi, and Lakshman and Shatrughna to Sumitra. The book details their upbringing, education in scriptures, and training in warfare. Notable events include Ram and Lakshman aiding Sage Vishwamitra by defeating demons that hindered his sacrifices. Ram, chosen by Vishwamitra, showed extraordinary valor at just 16 years old. The section also explores Sita’s divine origins—discovered in a field by King Janaka of Mithila—and her marriage to Ram, who proved his worth by lifting and stringing Lord Shiva’s bow. The Bala Kanda concludes with the weddings of Ram to Sita, Lakshman to Urmila, Bharat to Mandavi, and Shatrughna to Shrutakirti.

  • Ram and his brothers are born through a divine ritual.
  • Ram and Lakshman accompany Vishwamitra to vanquish demons.
  • Ram wins Sita's hand by lifting Shiva's bow.

Lesson: The Bala Kanda emphasizes humility, duty, and respect for elders.

Ayodhya Kanda

Summary: The Ayodhya Kanda chronicles the events leading to Lord Ram's exile to the forest for fourteen years. After twelve years of marriage, King Dasharath decides to crown Ram as his successor, a choice celebrated by the citizens and the royal assembly. However, on the eve of Ram's coronation, Kaikeyi, influenced by her scheming maid Manthara, demands two boons previously promised by Dasharath: Ram's exile for fourteen years and the throne for her son Bharat. Bound by his word, Dasharath reluctantly agrees. Ram, embodying humility and obedience, accepts his fate with grace. Despite Ram’s initial refusal, Sita insists on accompanying him, displaying her unwavering love and devotion. Lakshman also chooses to join them in exile. Bharat, upon learning of the deceit, refuses the throne and instead places Ram's sandals on it, ruling on Ram's behalf until his return.

  • Dasharath plans Ram's coronation, but Kaikeyi intervenes.
  • Ram, Sita, and Lakshman leave for the forest.
  • Bharat declines the throne and rules in Ram's name.

Lesson: Loyalty, selflessness, and adherence to dharma are central themes.

Aranya Kanda

Summary: The Aranya Kanda recounts pivotal events during the last year of Ram's exile. The narrative begins with Ram defeating Khara and his demon forces, retaliating against an attack triggered by Lakshman injuring Surpanakha, Ravana’s sister. The most significant event is Sita's abduction by Ravana after she steps outside the protective Lakshman Rekha to aid a disguised Ravana. Taken to Lanka, Sita resists Ravana’s advances and remains steadfast in her devotion to Ram. Meanwhile, Ram and Lakshman, learning of her abduction from the dying vulture Jatayu, set out in pursuit. Along the way, they encounter Kabandha and Shabari, who guide them to Sugriva and Hanuman, marking the beginning of a powerful alliance.

  • Ram defeats Khara and his demon army.
  • Sita is abducted by Ravana after crossing the Lakshman Rekha.
  • Ram and Lakshman meet Hanuman and Sugriva in their quest to rescue Sita.

Lesson: Courage, resilience, and devotion shine through in the face of adversity.

Kishkindha Kanda

Summary: The Kishkindha Kanda focuses on Ram’s alliance with the vanara king Sugriva. Ram and Lakshman meet Hanuman, Sugriva's loyal companion, who introduces them to Sugriva. Ram assists Sugriva by defeating his elder brother, Vali, enabling Sugriva to reclaim his throne. In return, Sugriva pledges to help Ram find Sita. Initially distracted from his promise, Sugriva is reminded by his wife Tara of his duty to Ram. Sugriva then dispatches search parties in all directions. The southern team, led by Hanuman and Angad, discovers from Sampati, a vulture, that Sita is imprisoned in Lanka.

  • Ram meets Hanuman and forms an alliance with Sugriva.
  • Sugriva regains his throne with Ram's help.
  • Hanuman learns of Sita’s location in Lanka.

Lesson: Friendship, loyalty, and perseverance are crucial for overcoming challenges.

Sundara Kanda

Summary: The Sundara Kanda is a vivid narration of Hanuman’s extraordinary journey to Lanka. Demonstrating his immense strength and devotion, Hanuman transforms into a colossal form to leap across the ocean. Facing numerous challenges from demons testing his power, Hanuman overcomes every obstacle to reach Lanka and locate Sita. His arrival causes chaos as he destroys trees, buildings, and Ravana’s soldiers. Intentionally captured to speak with Ravana, Hanuman appeals for Sita's release, but Ravana refuses and orders Hanuman’s tail to be set ablaze. Using this to his advantage, Hanuman leaps across rooftops, setting Ravana’s citadel on fire. Victorious, he returns to Kishkindha with news of Sita’s location.

  • Hanuman leaps across the ocean to Lanka.
  • He causes havoc in Ravana’s kingdom and meets Sita.
  • Hanuman returns with critical information for Ram.

Lesson: Devotion, courage, and resourcefulness lead to success.

Yuddha Kanda

Summary: The Yuddha Kanda details the climactic war between Ram and Ravana. The story begins with the construction of the Ram Setu bridge, built from floating stones inscribed with Ram’s name, allowing Ram’s army to cross to Lanka. Ravana’s brother Vibhishana allies with Ram. The epic also recounts Hanuman’s heroic act of carrying Mount Sumeru to save Lakshman, injured by a weapon wielded by Indrajit, Ravana’s son. The war culminates in Ram’s victory as he kills Ravana and crowns Vibhishana as Lanka’s king. Sita undergoes the Agni Pareeksha to prove her purity, emerging unscathed. With the war over and the exile completed, Ram returns to Ayodhya amidst grand celebrations.

  • Ram’s army builds the Ram Setu to reach Lanka.
  • Ram defeats Ravana, ending the war.
  • Sita proves her purity through the Agni Pareeksha.

Lesson: Triumph of good over evil and adherence to dharma.

Uttara Kanda

Summary: The Uttara Kanda narrates the later years of Ram, Sita, and their family. Despite proving her purity, Sita is exiled to the forest due to public opinion. Under the care of Sage Valmiki, she gives birth to twins, Lava and Kusha, who are raised with noble teachings. Valmiki composes the Ramyana, which Lava and Kusha recite before Ram. Overcome with sorrow upon hearing Sita’s story, Ram witnesses Sita’s return to the earth, her birthplace. Ram later learns Lava and Kusha are his sons. After fulfilling his earthly mission, Ram ascends to his heavenly abode, marking the end of his mortal journey.

  • Sita is exiled and gives birth to Lava and Kusha.
  • Valmiki composes the Ramyana, sung by Lava and Kusha.
  • Sita returns to the earth, and Ram ascends to Vaikuntha.

Lesson: The complexity of dharma and the inevitability of destiny are central themes.
