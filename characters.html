Main Characters

Ram

Role: Protagonist and seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu.

Attributes: Embodies righteousness, valor, and devotion to duty.

Significance: Undertakes the journey to rescue Sita and uphold dharma.

Sita

Role: Wife of Lord Ram and incarnation of Goddess Lakshmi.

Attributes: Represents purity, devotion, and patience.

Significance: Her abduction by Ravan sets the central conflict in motion.

Lakshman

Role: Younger brother of Lord Ram and loyal companion.

Attributes: Loyalty, bravery, and devotion to duty.

Significance: Protects Ram and Sita during their exile and fights alongside Ram.

Hanuman

Role: Devoted servant of Lord Ram and key ally in battle.

Attributes: Strength, loyalty, and intelligence.

Significance: Plays a crucial role in finding Sita and aiding in the defeat of Ravan.

Dasharath

Role: King of Ayodhya and father of Ram, Lakshman, Bharat, and Shatrughna.

Attributes: Noble and loving but bound by duty.

Significance: His vow to Queen Kaikeyi leads to Ram’s exile.

Kausalya

Role: Eldest queen of Dasharath and mother of Ram.

Attributes: Compassionate, noble, and wise.

Significance: Represents the ideal mother, deeply supports Ram even during his exile.

Sumitra

Role: Second queen of Dasharath and mother of Lakshman and Shatrughna.

Attributes: Wise, kind, and supportive.

Significance: Encourages Lakshman to accompany Ram, demonstrating her devotion to dharma.

Kaikeyi

Role: Dasharath’s third wife and Bharata’s mother.

Attributes: Initially loving but later manipulative.

Significance: Demands Ram’s exile to fulfill a boon, setting the events of the epic in motion.

Manthra

Role: Maid to Queen Kaikeyi and a key figure in Ramayana's conflict.

Attributes: Manipulative, shrewd, and cunning.

Significance: Persuades Kaikeyi to demand Ram's exile, triggering the epic’s central events.

Bharat

Role: Younger brother of Ram and caretaker of Ayodhya.

Attributes: Humility, loyalty, and selflessness.

Significance: Refuses to rule in Ram’s absence and acts as a steward.

Shatrughna

Role: Youngest brother of Ram.

Attributes: Dutiful and supportive.

Significance: Assists Bharat in managing the kingdom during Ram’s exile.

Vibhishan

Role: Younger brother of Ravan and ally of Ram.

Attributes: Righteous and wise.

Significance: Defects to Ram’s side, aiding in Ravan’s defeat.

Sugriva

Role: The king of the vanaras (monkey army).

Attributes: Friendship and leadership.

Significance: Helps Ram in the search for Sita and provides the vanara army for the battle against Ravan.

Jatayu

Role: Divine bird and friend of Dasharath.

Attributes: Valor and loyalty.

Significance: Sacrifices his life trying to save Sita from Ravan.

Ravan

Role: Antagonist and king of Lanka.

Attributes: Great power, intellect, and arrogance.

Significance: Abducts Sita, leading to the climactic battle with Lord Ram.

Kumbhakarna

Role: Ravan’s giant brother.

Attributes: Strength and loyalty to family.

Significance: Fights Ram despite knowing Ravan is wrong.

Surpanakha

Role: Ravan’s sister.

Attributes: Spiteful and jealous.

Significance: Her humiliation sparks Ravan’s desire for revenge.
